Configure Custom Domain

Setup Guide
1
Own a domain name
You need a domain you control (e.g. panel.yourdomain.com).
Buy one from Namecheap, GoDaddy, Cloudflare, or any registrar.
2
Point DNS to your machine
Add an A record in your DNS provider's dashboard:
Type: A
Name: panel
Value: (your public IP)
TTL: 300 (5 minutes)
This creates panel.yourdomain.com pointing to your Windows machine's public IP.
3
Verify DNS propagation
Enter your domain below and click Check DNS to confirm it resolves to your machine.
4
Open firewall ports
Windows Firewall must allow inbound connections on ports 80 (HTTP) and 443 (HTTPS).
netsh advfirewall firewall add rule name="PteroWindows HTTP" dir=in action=allow protocol=TCP localport=80
netsh advfirewall firewall add rule name="PteroWindows HTTPS" dir=in action=allow protocol=TCP localport=443
5
Forward ports from your router (if needed)
If your Windows machine is behind a router, forward ports 80 and 443 TCP to its local IP (e.g. 192.168.1.100).
Finding your local IP: Run ipconfig in Command Prompt — look for "IPv4 Address" under your active adapter.
6
Test port reachability
Verify ports 80 and 443 are reachable from the internet.
7
Apply domain to PteroWindows
Enter your domain and optionally your email for Let's Encrypt SSL below, then click Apply Domain. The panel will restart with the new domain.
APP_URL-
LE_EMAIL-
8
Verify SSL certificate
After the panel restarts, verify that the SSL certificate was provisioned correctly. Let's Encrypt auto-provisions when it detects ports 80 and 443 are open.
